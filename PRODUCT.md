# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Mixed audience: hobbyist homelabbers (self-taught, self-hosting enthusiasts tinkering with Docker) and professional developers (engineers evaluating tools for work or personal projects). Content must serve both without condescending to either.

## Product Purpose

A bilingual (EN/zh-CN) technical blog providing deep-dive installation guides, honest comparisons, and real-world troubleshooting logs for open-source, self-hosted, and AI tools. Success means readers save hours by reading HardStack instead of stitching together five forum threads.

## Brand Personality

sharp, minimalist, technical.

Voice is confident but not arrogant — an expert peer who's been through the errors and taken the screenshots. Editorial-caliber writing with a hands-on, no-armchair attitude. Stratechery's text-first, chrome-light editorial clarity is the north star.

## Anti-references

- Cluttered "tech blog" templates with sidebars, popups, and widget bloat
- Over-decorated AI-generated design (gradient text, glassmorphism, numbered section markers by default, side-stripe borders)
- Generic Medium clone or corporate SaaS landing page aesthetics
- Anything that prioritizes "looking designed" over readability

## Design Principles

1. **Text-first, chrome-second** — layout, color, and motion exist to serve reading and focus. Every pixel of chrome must earn its place against the baseline: no chrome at all.
2. **Bilingual by design** — English and Chinese are co-equal citizens. Typography, spacing, and layout must accommodate both gracefully (Latin and CJC glyphs side by side).
3. **Expert confidence without gatekeeping** — technical depth is uncompromised, but the tone never shames the reader. Walk the line between "this is hard" and "you can do it."
4. **Honest craft** — the design communicates the same hands-on integrity as the content. No stock photos, no polished-over errors, no publishing without follow-up.
5. **Built to last** — static-first, minimal JS dependency, fast loads. Design decisions respect the self-hosted ethos: own your stack, optimize for the reader, not for engagement metrics.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA target. Minimum 4.5:1 contrast for body text, 3:1 for large text. Full keyboard navigation, skip-to-content links, screen-reader-accessible interactive elements (search, locale switcher). Reduced-motion compatible animations.
